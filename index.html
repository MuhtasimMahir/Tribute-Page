Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman

Father of a Nation

Here's a timeline of Sheikh Mujib

  • 1920 - Born in Tungipara, Bangladesh
  • 1943 - He joined the Bengal Muslim League
  • 1946 - Mujib was one of the Muslim politicians working under Suhrawardy during the communal violence that broke out in Calcutta
  • 1947 - Got his BA degree
  • 1948 - Sheikh Mujibur was arrested in front of the secretariat building
  • 1950 - Awamin Muslim League held an anti-famine procession in Dhaka on the occasion of the arrival of Pakistan's Prime Minister Liaquat Ali Khan
  • 1954 - He was made the party's general secretary, and elected to the East Bengal Legislative Assembly on a United Front coalition ticket
  • 1956 - Mujib entered a second coalition government as minister of industries, commerce, labour, anti-corruption and village aid.
  • 1958 - General Ayub Khan suspended the constitution and imposed martial law.
  • 1961 - Mujib was arrested for organizing resistance and imprisoned
  • 1969 - Dropped the charges on 22 February and unconditionally released Mujib the following day
  • 1970 - The Awami League under Mujib's leadership won a massive majority in the provincial legislature
  • 1971 - 7 March Mujib called for independence and asked the people to launch a major campaign of civil disobedience and organized armed resistance at a mass gathering of people held at the Ramna Race Course Ground in Dhaka
  • 1975 - A group of junior army officers invaded the presidential residence with tanks and killed Mujib, his family and personal staff.
